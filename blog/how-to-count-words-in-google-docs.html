How to Count Words in Google Docs: A Complete Guide

Whether you're a student writing an essay, a professional preparing a report, or a content creator crafting your next piece, knowing how to count words in Google Docs is an essential skill. In this comprehensive guide, we'll cover all the methods available to track your word count.

Using the Built-in Word Count Tool

Google Docs provides a built-in word counter that's always visible at the bottom of your document. Here's how to find it:

  1. Open your document in Google Docs
  2. Look at the bottom left corner of your screen
  3. You'll see the word count displayed (e.g., "1,234 words")

Click on this number to open a detailed word count panel that shows page count, character count (with and without spaces), and paragraph count.

Keyboard Shortcuts for Quick Word Counting

For power users who want instant word count information, these keyboard shortcuts come in handy:

  • Ctrl + Shift + C (Windows) or Cmd + Shift + C (Mac): Opens the word count panel
  • Ctrl + A then Ctrl + Shift + C: Count words in a specific selection

Word Count in Google Docs Mobile App

Working on the go? The Google Docs mobile app also shows word count:

  1. Open your document in the Google Docs app
  2. Tap the three-dot menu in the top right
  3. Select "Word count" to see your document statistics

Setting Word Count Goals

Google Docs allows you to set writing goals:

  1. Go to Tools > Word count
  2. Check the box for "Automatically display word count"
  3. Set your target word count

Your word count will now be visible as you type, helping you stay on track with your writing goals.
