Character Count Limits for Social Media Posts in 2024

Social media platforms each have their own character limits that can make or break your post's effectiveness. Understanding these limits is crucial for crafting messages that resonate with your audience. Here's the complete guide to social media character limits.

Twitter (X) Character Limits

280 characters - Twitter's iconic limit that encourages concise, impactful messages.

  • Links (URLs) count toward your character limit
  • Images and videos don't count toward the limit
  • Quote tweets preserve the original text count

LinkedIn Character Limits

3000 characters for posts - LinkedIn offers more room for professional content:

  • Feed posts: 3000 characters
  • Comments: 3000 characters
  • Personal bio/headline: 220 characters
  • Company bio: 2000 characters

Facebook Character Limits

63,206 characters - While Facebook allows long posts, shorter posts typically perform better:

  • Recommended: 40-80 characters for maximum engagement
  • Links with thumbnails get more attention
  • Video descriptions: 255 characters

Instagram Character Limits

2,200 characters for captions - But the first 125 characters show in feed previews:

  • Caption: 2,200 characters
  • Bio: 150 characters
  • Hashtags: Up to 30 hashtags (recommended: 9)
  • Comments: 2,200 characters

Best Practices for Social Media Limits

  • Twitter: Front-load your message - put the most important words first
  • LinkedIn: Use the extra space wisely with structured, professional content
  • Facebook: Keep it short and sweet for better engagement
  • Instagram: Put key info in the first 125 characters
